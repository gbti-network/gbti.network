> **Lume Web submits its [2023 year in review](https://forum.sia.tech/t/standard-grant-proposal-lume-web-2024/414) to the Sia Foundation’s grant department and makes its case for additional funding for 2024.**
> 
> [Tweet](/)

[Derrick Hammer](https://www.linkedin.com/in/derrickhammer), aka @pcfreak30, announces significant advancements in the flagship project, [Lume Web](https://lumeweb.com/), to mark a new era in decentralized web access and data sovereignty.

By overcoming challenges faced by [the collapse of Skynet](https://gbti.network/blockchain/the-collapse-of-skynet-what-happened/), Lume Web has redefined their approach, focusing on creating a user-owned, open web environment. This initiative aligns perfectly with the [Sia Foundation](https://sia.tech/about-sia-foundation)’s mission to promote user-owned data.

## Key Achievements and Innovations:

-   **Robust Technology Integration:** Lume Web successfully integrated support for [Handshake](https://handshake.org/), [Ethereum](https://ethereum.org/en/) and [IPFS](https://ipfs.tech/) networks into its routing technology. This crucial step towards interoperability has led to the creation of a functional Lume prototype.
-   **Lume Portal Development:** The introduction of the Lume Portal, a Layer 2 solution, bridges gaps in Sia’s ecosystem post-Skynet. This backend server, crucial for app delivery, reflects the project’s commitment to decentralized access and data handling.
-   **User-Focused Products:** Despite challenges with its Firefox extension, Lume Web leveraged the feedback to innovate further. The development of a web3 browser app and an NFT gallery app showcases Lume’s versatility in integrating Sia’s [renterd](https://docs.sia.tech/renting/about-renting) technology.
-   **Community Engagement:** The upcoming community platform, web3.news, will serve as a hub for industry-wide collaboration, emphasizing the project’s community-first approach.

## Looking Ahead: 2024 Objectives

-   **Superportal Software Development:** Lume Web aims to enhance its portal software to support multiple web3 protocols, starting with [S5](https://forum.sia.tech/t/grant-proposal-s5-network-and-apps/305#so-what-is-s5-6) and IPFS.
-   **User Interface Enhancement:** The project will focus on developing a user-friendly dashboard and a dedicated marketing site.
-   **Kernel System and Relay Server Upgrades:** Upcoming upgrades to these systems will improve efficiency and functionality.
-   **Expanding Community Services:** The ongoing development of web3.news and the introduction of a robust community hosting service are key goals for the coming year.

## Impact on Stakeholders:

-   **Sia Community:** Lume Web’s advancements offer the Sia community a versatile L2 portal software, aiding in data hosting and network growth.
-   **Data Hosters and DApps/Web Apps:** The project provides decentralized storage solutions and supports web app hosting on the Sia network, championing censorship resistance and data democratization.

**Funding and Development Outlook:**

-   Lume Web has proposed a total budget of $91,000, with detailed allocations for development, marketing, and infrastructure enhancements. The project commits to open-source development, ensuring transparency and community involvement.

**Risks and Mitigation Strategies:**

-   The Lume Web team has identified potential risks, including technical challenges with the S5 development and third-party integrations. They remain committed to addressing these challenges proactively.

**Commitment to Progress:**

-   With monthly progress reports, the team at Hammer Technologies LLC pledges to maintain transparency and adaptability, ensuring Lume Web continues to be at the forefront of decentralized web technology.
